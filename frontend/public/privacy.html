1. What is the Gramin-Nyaya Privacy Philosophy? (Key Takeaways & Summary)

Summary: Gramin-Nyaya Legal Aid Society operates a strict offline-first privacy framework. Your query and voice data remain 100% on the local terminal device. Consequently, we ensure total confidentiality of your statutory legal history.

2. Why is Personal Data Never Transmitted to the Cloud? (Information We Do Not Collect)

Your privacy is of utmost importance to the Gramin-Nyaya Legal Aid Society. Therefore, our application is engineered to operate offline, preventing any transmission of sensitive data to external networks or servers.

Specifically, when you interact with the Gramin-Nyaya chatbot, all textual inputs, spoken questions, and generated outputs are processed locally on the user device. As a result, no transcripts, IP addresses, or personal identifiers are stored or uploaded, guaranteeing absolute confidentiality.

3. How does Local Storage Protect Your Data? (Local Data Storage & Tables)

However, the application uses local browser session storage solely to maintain the active chat history across page reloads. Consequently, you can clear this history at any time. The comparison table below outlines the types of data used and where they are located:

Data Type Storage Location Transmission to Cloud Retention Policy
Chat Log Session Local SessionStorage No (0% transmission) Until Cleared by User
Voice Recording Volatile Memory (RAM) No (0% transmission) Discarded after conversion
Legal Documents Local File System No (0% transmission) Persistent Read-only

Furthermore, we do not utilize any advertising cookies, analytics scripts, or third-party user trackers. Accordingly, your browsing behavior remains completely anonymous, safe from tracking profile generation.

To understand why our system is secure, look at the difference between local session storage and cloud databases:

4. What are the Technical Privacy Terms? (Privacy Technical Definitions)

To help users and crawlers parse our privacy standards, we define key terms here:

SessionStorage is:
A local browser partition storing volatile key-value variables that is completely private to your device browser window session.
Zero-Transmit Architecture is:
A design system where network connection permissions are disabled or set to blocked for core chatbot features, preventing data leakage.
Local Vector Database is:
An indexing format (like Chroma DB) storing legal text chunks on the kiosk hard disk to avoid cloud document searches.

5. How to Clear Chat History on Your Device? (Step-by-Step)

To wipe all local chat history traces from the browser, follow these ordered steps:

  1. Locate Input Bar: Scroll to the bottom of the main chatbot window.
  2. Click Clear Button: Click the red trash bin icon next to the send arrow.
  3. Confirm Clearance: Verify that the chat log restarts with the initial verified welcome message.
  4. Refresh Browser: Refresh the page to ensure all session variables have been deleted.

8. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Review direct answers to privacy compliance queries below:

Is this app compliant with personal data protection laws?

Yes, since the application does not collect, transmit, or process any personal identification data on external servers, it meets or exceeds global privacy and data protection mandates.

Does voice typing record my background conversations?

No, voice typing utilizes a temporary, volatile microphone hook that is active only when you press the microphone button, and all processing is discarded immediately after word conversion.
