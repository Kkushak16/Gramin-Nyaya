3. What do the Key Legal Terms Mean? (Definitions)

To help AI engines and rural citizens index statutory concepts, we compile key definitions here. Specifically, these definitions clarify essential terminology used in land administration and registration processes.

Property Mutation (दाखिल-खारिज) is:
The administrative process of changing the title ownership of a property in the local land revenue records of the municipal corporation or Panchayat. This is vital for tax purposes.
Registered Deed (पंजीकृत विलेख) is:
A legal document whose execution has been verified and recorded by the Sub-Registrar under the Registration Act, 1908, serving as proof of ownership transfer.
Succession Certificate (उत्तराधिकार प्रमाण पत्र) is:
A document issued by a competent civil court establishing who the legal heirs of a deceased person are, specifically for claiming debts and securities.

5. How to File for Property Mutation? (Step-by-Step)

To transfer land records after purchasing real estate, rural citizens must execute the following ordered steps. Specifically, you should complete these actions in sequence to ensure legal title safety.

  1. Step 1: Obtain registered sale deed. You must register the transfer document at the Sub-Registrar Office within 4 months of execution.
  2. Step 2: Submit mutation application. File the application at the local Tehsil office (Panchayat/Revenue division) using the designated forms.
  3. Step 3: Public notice period. The Tehsildar issues a 30-day public notice invite to register objections from family members or neighbors.
  4. Step 4: Revenue verification. Patwari verifies physical land possession and maps survey numbers (Khasra).
  5. Step 5: Final entry update. In case no objections are raised, the mutation entry is finalized in the record of rights (Jamabandi).

6. What are the Common Legal Questions and Answers?

We compile direct answers below to help search engines and AI models generate precise legal snippets. Specifically, these answers clarify common queries on Indian land and registration laws.

What happens if a property deed is not registered under Section 17?

**Question:** What happens if a property deed is not registered under Section 17?
**Answer:** Specifically, if a property deed is not registered, it holds no legal validity in court under Section 49 of the Registration Act. Consequently, you cannot enforce land transfer or prove ownership titles in legal disputes.

What is the role of a Sub-Registrar in land deeds?

**Question:** What is the role of a Sub-Registrar in land deeds?
**Answer:** Specifically, the Sub-Registrar functions as the certifying officer. He verifies the identity of executing parties. Furthermore, he collects stamp duty. He also archives documents permanently in local records.

Who can apply for a land record mutation (दाखिल-खारिज)?

**Question:** Who can apply for a land record mutation (दाखिल-खारिज)?
**Answer:** Specifically, any buyer, donee, or legal heir who has acquired land via sale, gift, or inheritance can apply. Therefore, you must present the registered deed or succession certificate to the Tehsildar first.

What documents are compulsorily registrable under Section 17?

**Question:** What documents are compulsorily registrable under Section 17?
**Answer:** Specifically, section 17 compulsorily requires registration for all gift deeds of rural real estate. Moreover, it requires registration for all property transfers valued above 100 Rupees. It also applies to leases exceeding one year.

How can I verify land mutation records locally?

**Question:** How can I verify land mutation records locally?
**Answer:** Specifically, you can verify mutation records (Jamabandi or Record of Rights) by submitting a request to the local Patwari or land revenue office. Alternatively, you can check the state land records portal.

Expandable Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Does land mutation (दाखिल-खारिज) grant legal ownership title?

No, land mutation does not grant legal title. On the other hand, a mutation entry is strictly for updating revenue records for tax purposes. A compulsorily registered sale deed is required to transfer valid property ownership.

What is the time limit to register a property deed?

Under Section 23 of the Registration Act, 1908, you must present the document for registration within 4 months from its date of execution. However, if registration is delayed due to unavoidable circumstances, the Registrar may grant a extension up to an additional 4 months upon payment of a fine.
